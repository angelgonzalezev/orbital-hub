# List your startup

Listing on Orbital is free. You create a draft, pass a short checklist, request verification, and a human reviews your submission before it goes live on the [marketplace](https://orbitalhub.dev/startups).

If you have not signed in yet, start with [Getting started](/guide/getting-started).

## Create a draft

A draft holds everything a visitor sees on your startup page:

| Field          | Details                                                                                |
| -------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Basics         | Name, logo, a one-liner up to 160 characters, and a description up to 2,000 characters |
| Location       | Geocoded — this is what places your startup on The Orbital, the 3D globe               |
| Links          | Website, X, Discord, GitHub                                                            |
| Stage          | Idea, MVP, Early-stage, Scaling, or Established                                        |
| Team size      | Headcount                                                                              |
| MRR            | Optional. You choose whether it is shown publicly or kept private                      |
| Market signals | "Raising funds" and "Open to acquisition"                                              |
| Team           | Search registered Orbital users and add them as collaborators with roles               |
| Categories     | 1 to 5, across Sectors, Infra and Tech, and Services                                   |
| Technologies   | Up to 10 from the tech-stack list                                                      |

You can save and return to a draft as often as you like before requesting verification.

## Market signals

The "Raising funds" and "Open to acquisition" signals are filters in the marketplace, so investors and acquirers browsing Orbital can find founders who are open to those conversations. Turning one on tells visitors you welcome the approach.

::: warning Signals start conversations — nothing more
Market signals are self-declared by founders. Orbital does not process investments or acquisitions, hold funds, or verify fundraising claims. A startup marked as open to acquisition conversations is exactly that: a founder saying they are willing to talk. Where this is heading is covered in [Vision](/vision).
:::

## The verification checklist

Before you can request verification, your listing must pass a 7-point checklist:

1. A complete profile with display name and job title
2. A description of at least 200 characters
3. A website
4. An X profile
5. At least 1 category
6. At least 1 team member
7. All fields within their limits

The checklist is shown after every save, so you always know what is left.

## Human review

Every submission is reviewed by a human, who approves or rejects it. There is no automated pass. Once approved, your startup is published to the marketplace and appears on The Orbital, and Orbital posts an announcement about your launch on X.

::: info On our roadmap
Automated domain and X ownership verification is planned to strengthen the process. Review stays human either way — approval is never fully automatic.
:::

## Listing lifecycle

A listing moves through four states: **draft → pending review → verified → published**.

A few rules to know:

- Editing your website or X link after verification resets the listing to unverified, since those links are part of what was reviewed. Other edits do not.
- You can archive a published startup at any time, which removes it from the marketplace.
- Archived startups can be permanently deleted.

## After publishing

Your work is not done at publish. Featured placement puts your startup at the top of the marketplace and gives it a gold marker on the globe — see [Featured listings](/guide/featured-listings). And make sure your X or Telegram handle is set on your profile, since that is how people reach you.
