# Discover startups

The marketplace at [orbitalhub.dev/startups](https://orbitalhub.dev/startups) is publicly browsable. No account is required — anyone can search, filter, and open any published startup.

## Filters

The marketplace supports five ways to narrow the list:

- **Free-text search** across startup names and descriptions.
- **Categories**, organized in three groups (see below).
- **Stage**, from idea to established.
- **Tech stack**, any of 19 technologies.
- **Market signals**: "Raising funds" and "Open to acquisition". These surface founders who have marked their startup as raising funds or as open to acquisition conversations.

Market signals are self-declared by founders. Orbital does not verify fundraising claims or endorse listings — treat every signal as an invitation to start a conversation and do your own diligence.

[Featured startups](/guide/featured-listings) sort first in every view.

## The startup detail page

Every published startup has a public page with:

- **About** — the description and links the founder provided.
- **Tech stack and categories** — the taxonomy tags above.
- **Key metrics** — stage, location, team size, MRR (only if the founder made it public), and launch date.
- **Team** — the people behind the startup.
- **Founder contact** — direct links to reach out.

## Contacting founders

Contact happens off-platform: each startup page links out to the founder's X or Telegram. This is a deliberate choice — no in-app inbox means no unread queue for founders to babysit, and conversations start where founders already are.

::: info Today vs. roadmap
There is no in-app messaging today. Structured intro requests and messaging are planned — see [Messaging](/about/messaging) for the reasoning and the [Roadmap](/roadmap) for where it fits.
:::

## The Orbital

[orbitalhub.dev/orbital](https://orbitalhub.dev/orbital) renders every geo-located published startup on a 3D globe — a clearer view of where things are being built across Solana. Featured startups carry a gold marker. Click any marker to open the startup's detail page.
